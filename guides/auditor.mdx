## Overview

As a CPA Auditor (CPA Revisor), you play a critical role in ensuring financial accountability and proper use of funds across all communities in Auditoriapp. This guide covers your review and approval workflows.

## Your Responsibilities

CPA Auditors are responsible for:

- **Reviewing and approving projects** submitted by communities
- **Validating expense reports** (rendiciones) for accuracy and compliance
- **Issuing observations** when corrections are needed
- **Rejecting non-compliant submissions**
- **Monitoring financial execution** across all communities
- **Ensuring proper documentation** for all transactions

## Access & Permissions

Your role (`cpa` or `CPA Revisor`) provides:

- **Cross-community visibility**: View all communities' projects and expenses
- **Approval authority**: Only CPAs can approve projects and rendiciones
- **Observation powers**: Issue observations requiring corrections
- **Status change control**: Manage project workflow transitions
- **Financial oversight**: Access to complete financial data

<Warning>
You have elevated permissions across the entire platform. Exercise caution when reviewing and approving submissions.
</Warning>

## Understanding the Multi-Tenant System

Auditoriapp uses logical multi-tenancy:

- Each **Comunidad** (community) has isolated data
- Communities cannot see each other's information
- As a CPA, you can view **all communities** for oversight
- Each project and expense is tied to a specific community
- Budget periods are community-specific

## Project Review Workflow

### Understanding Project States

Projects flow through these states:

1. **Borrador** (Draft): Initial community creation
2. **Revisión Interna** (Internal Review): Community internal validation
3. **Revisión CPA** (CPA Review): **Your review stage**
4. **Observaciones** (With Observations): Returned for corrections
5. **Aprobado** (Approved): **You approved** - ready for execution
6. **Ejecución** (In Execution): Project implementation
7. **Cierre** (Closed): Project completed

### Accessing Projects for Review

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Projects">
    Click **Proyectos** in the main navigation to view all projects across communities.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Filter by Status">
    Use the status filter to show only projects in **Revisión CPA** status that require your review.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select a Project">
    Click on a project to view full details and begin your review.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Reviewing a Project

<Steps>
  <Step title="Verify Basic Information">
    Review the project fundamentals:
    - **Nombre** and **Descripción**: Clear and specific
    - **Fechas**: Realistic start and end dates
    - **Presupuesto Total**: Reasonable and within period allocation
    - **Comunidad**: Verify community identity
    - **Periodo**: Confirm active period
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Review Strategic Content">
    Assess the project formulation:
    - **Objetivos**: Are objectives clear and measurable?
    - **Justificación**: Is the need well-documented?
    - **Beneficiarios Estimados**: Reasonable number of beneficiaries?
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Validate Governance Requirements">
    Check compliance with governance:
    - **Quorum Asamblea**: Sufficient attendance number
    - **Firma Presidente**: President approval confirmed
    - **Fecha Firma Presidente**: Approval date recorded
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Review Documentation">
    Verify all required documents are present:
    - **Acta de Asamblea**: Assembly minutes uploaded
    - **Cotizaciones**: Multiple vendor quotes (usually 3)
    - **Cotización Elegida**: Selected quote clearly marked
    - **Document quality**: PDFs are clear and complete
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Check Budget Alignment">
    Verify financial aspects:
    - Budget within period allocation
    - No duplicate projects
    - Costs align with quotes
    - Calculation accuracy
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Review Project History">
    Check the status change history:
    - Previous reviewers' comments
    - Past observations and resolutions
    - Timeline of submissions
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Approving a Project

If the project meets all requirements:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click Change Status">
    In the project details page, click the **Cambiar Estado** button.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select Approved Status">
    Choose **Aprobado** from the status dropdown.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Add Approval Comments">
    Optionally add comments:
    - Confirmation of approval
    - Any conditions or notes
    - Recommendations for execution
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Confirm Approval">
    Click **Confirmar** to approve the project. The community can now begin execution.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
Only users with the `cpa` role can approve projects. This is enforced at the API level.
</Note>

### Issuing Observations

When a project needs corrections:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Identify Issues">
    Document all issues found during review:
    - Missing or incomplete information
    - Incorrect calculations
    - Missing documents
    - Governance non-compliance
    - Budget issues
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Change Status to Observations">
    Click **Cambiar Estado** and select **Observaciones**.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Write Clear Observations">
    In the comments field, provide detailed, actionable feedback:
    
    **Good observation example**:
    ```
    OBSERVACIONES:
    1. Falta acta de asamblea - debe incluir acta firmada
    2. Presupuesto ($5,000,000) excede el monto asignado del período ($4,500,000)
    3. Justificación insuficiente - ampliar explicación de la necesidad
    4. Cotizaciones: solo se adjuntan 2 cotizaciones, se requieren mínimo 3
    ```
    
    **Poor observation example**:
    ```
    Revisar documentos
    ```
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Submit Observations">
    Click **Confirmar** to send the project back to the community with your observations.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Rejecting a Project

For projects that should not proceed:

<Note>
Rejection is typically done by changing to "Observaciones" status rather than a separate "Rechazado" state. The community can then decide whether to correct or abandon the project.
</Note>

## Expense Review Workflow

### Understanding Expense States

Rendiciones (expense reports) have these states:

- **Pendiente** (Pending): Awaiting your review
- **Aprobado** (Approved): You validated the expense
- **Observado** (With Observations): Requires corrections
- **Rechazado** (Rejected): Not approved
- **Pagado** (Paid): Payment processed (after approval)

### Accessing Expenses for Review

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Rendiciones">
    Click **Rendiciones** in the main menu or access from within a project.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Filter by Status">
    Filter to show **Pendiente** status to see expenses awaiting review.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Filter by Community (Optional)">
    If reviewing a specific community, apply the community filter.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Reviewing an Expense Report

<Steps>
  <Step title="Verify Basic Information">
    Check the expense fundamentals:
    - **Proyecto**: Confirm project exists and is approved
    - **Monto Rendido**: Amount is reasonable
    - **Número Documento**: Invoice/receipt number present
    - **Fecha Rendición**: Date is logical
    - **Descripción**: Clear description of expense
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Review Supporting Documents">
    Examine uploaded documentation:
    - Invoice or receipt is attached
    - Document is legible and complete
    - Amount matches the rendición
    - Vendor information is clear
    - Date on document matches report date
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Validate Against Project Budget">
    Check budget compliance:
    - Expense is within project budget
    - Sum of all rendiciones doesn't exceed project total
    - Expense aligns with project purpose
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Verify Eligibility">
    Ensure the expense is appropriate:
    - Relates to approved project activities
    - Vendor/supplier is legitimate
    - No duplicate expenses
    - Complies with funding rules
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Approving an Expense

<Steps>
  <Step title="Access Review Action">
    In the rendición details or list view, click **Revisar**.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select Approved Status">
    Choose **Aprobado** from the status options.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Add Review Comments (Optional)">
    Provide any notes about the approval:
    - Confirmation
    - Conditions
    - Notes for payment processing
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Submit Approval">
    Click **Confirmar** to approve the expense. The system records:
    - Your user ID as reviewer
    - Timestamp of review
    - New status
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Issuing Observations on Expenses

When an expense needs clarification or correction:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click Review">
    Access the review interface for the rendición.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select Observado Status">
    Choose **Observado** (With Observations).
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Write Clear Observations">
    Provide specific, actionable feedback:
    
    **Good observation examples**:
    ```
    - Factura ilegible, favor resubir documento escaneado en alta calidad
    - Monto no coincide: factura indica $150,000 pero rendición declara $180,000
    - Falta información del proveedor en la factura
    - Gasto no relacionado con objetivos del proyecto aprobado
    ```
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Submit Observations">
    Click **Confirmar**. The community will be notified to review and address your comments.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Rejecting an Expense

For expenses that should not be approved:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Access Review">
    Click **Revisar** on the rendición.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select Rejected Status">
    Choose **Rechazado**.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Provide Rejection Reason">
    Explain clearly why the expense is rejected:
    - Non-compliance with rules
    - Fraudulent documentation
    - Duplicate submission
    - Outside project scope
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Confirm Rejection">
    Submit the rejection. This expense will not be reimbursed.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
Rejections are permanent for that specific rendición. The community must submit a new, corrected rendición if appropriate.
</Warning>

- **Total projects** across all communities
- **Projects by status** (pending review, approved, etc.)
- **Expense statistics** (pending, approved, observed)
- **Budget execution** across communities
- **Alert indicators** for items requiring attention

- **Rendiciones Pendientes**: Expenses awaiting your review
- **Rendiciones Observadas**: Expenses with unresolved observations
- **Proyectos en Revisión CPA**: Projects waiting for your approval
- **Proyectos Atrasados**: Projects past deadline without closure
- **Budget overruns**: Projects exceeding allocated budgets

## Review Best Practices

### Documentation Standards

- **Always verify document authenticity** - look for signs of tampering
- **Check document completeness** - all required fields present
- **Ensure legibility** - text and numbers must be clear
- **Verify dates** - logical sequence of events
- **Cross-reference** - amounts match across documents

### Communication Guidelines

- **Be specific** in observations - avoid vague comments
- **Be constructive** - help communities improve
- **Be timely** - review submissions promptly
- **Be consistent** - apply same standards across communities
- **Document everything** - use comment fields thoroughly

### Approval Principles

- **Verify before approving** - don't rush reviews
- **Request clarification** when in doubt
- **Maintain independence** - objective review only
- **Follow procedures** - comply with audit standards
- **Track patterns** - identify recurring issues

### Risk Management

- **Flag unusual patterns**: Multiple similar expenses, round numbers
- **Verify vendor diversity**: Not all expenses to same vendor
- **Check timeline logic**: Expense dates align with project timeline
- **Monitor budget consumption**: Sudden spikes in spending
- **Review related projects**: Potential splitting of expenses

### Bulk Review Considerations

<Note>
The current system requires individual review of each project and expense. Plan your review sessions accordingly.
</Note>

For efficient bulk reviews:

1. **Group by community**: Review all items from one community together
2. **Group by type**: Review all projects, then all expenses
3. **Prioritize**: Handle urgent or time-sensitive items first
4. **Set schedules**: Regular review times (e.g., every Tuesday and Friday)

## Troubleshooting

### Cannot Approve Project

**Issue**: Approval button disabled or error when approving

**Solution**:
- Verify project is in "Revisión CPA" status
- Confirm you're logged in with CPA role
- Check that project hasn't already been approved
- Refresh the page and try again

### Missing Documents

**Issue**: Project shows documents uploaded but can't view them

**Solution**:
- Check your browser allows pop-ups from the domain
- Verify document IDs are present in project data
- Contact system administrator if documents are truly missing
- Request community to re-upload if necessary

### Status Change Failed

**Issue**: Error message when changing project or expense status

**Solution**:
- Verify you have CPA permissions
- Check the current status allows the transition you're attempting
- Ensure you've filled required comment fields
- Check network connection

### Cannot See All Communities

**Issue**: Only seeing one or limited communities

**Solution**:
- Verify your user role is set to `cpa` or `CPA Revisor`
- Confirm you're not filtering by a specific community
- Check with administrator that your user is not assigned to a specific community
- Re-login to refresh permissions

## Reporting Issues

If you discover:

- **Financial irregularities**: Document thoroughly and escalate immediately
- **System bugs**: Report to technical support with screenshots
- **Recurring community issues**: Provide feedback for training
- **Process improvements**: Suggest enhancements to workflows

## Compliance & Audit Trail

The system maintains complete audit trails:

- **All status changes** are logged with user and timestamp
- **Document uploads** are tracked
- **Review actions** are permanently recorded
- **Comments and observations** are preserved
- **Financial transactions** have complete history

This ensures accountability and enables future audits.
